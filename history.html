History of Skikda

A journey through time

3rd century BC

Foundation of Rusicade

Founded by the Phoenicians as a trading post, Rusicade becomes an important Roman city after the Roman conquest of North Africa.

1st - 3rd century

Roman Peak

Under the Roman Empire, Rusicade reached its peak with impressive monuments: a theater, temples, baths, and a sophisticated aqueduct system.

5th - 7th century

Byzantine Period

After the fall of the Western Roman Empire, the region falls under Byzantine control with Byzantine churches marking the Christianization of the region.

7th century

Arab Conquest

Muslim armies conquer the region, introducing Islam and Arabic culture. The city becomes known as Skikda, derived from the Berber word 'Tasikida' meaning 'market'.

16th - 19th century

Ottoman Era

Skikda becomes part of the Ottoman Empire, serving as a small port town. The region sees the construction of Ottoman-style buildings and mosques.

1838

French Colonization

The French establish Philippeville (modern-day Skikda) as a colonial city, developing the port and constructing European-style buildings.

1962

Algerian Independence

After Algeria gains independence from France, the city officially returns to its original name Skikda and becomes an important industrial and port city.
